What Safe Tattooing Looks Like

TATTOO SAFETY IN NASHVILLE what to check before you sit down

Tennessee requires tattoo artists to be licensed and shops to pass health department inspections. These requirements set a baseline — but the difference between a technically compliant shop and a genuinely safe, professional environment is visible before the first needle touches skin. Knowing what to look for protects you.

What to Look For

THE SAFETY CHECKLIST before you commit to any Nashville shop

01

SINGLE-USE NEEDLES — NON-NEGOTIABLE

Every needle used in your session should be opened from sealed packaging in front of you and disposed of immediately after use. Never accept a session where needles are reused or where you cannot verify they were opened fresh. This is the most basic safety requirement in tattooing and the one that most directly protects your health.

02

GLOVES THROUGHOUT THE SESSION

Your artist should wear gloves for the entire session — from setup through cleanup. Gloves should not be removed and replaced with ungloved hands touching your tattoo at any point. If an artist removes gloves mid-session to handle something else and then continues without changing gloves, this is a health violation.

03

AUTOCLAVE-STERILIZED EQUIPMENT

Reusable equipment — grips, tubes, and any metal components — should be sterilized in an autoclave between clients. Ask to see the autoclave and ask when equipment was last sterilized. A professional shop will answer this without hesitation. Hesitation is itself informative.

04

VISIBLE CLEANLINESS

The station should be clean before your session begins. Surfaces should be covered with fresh barrier film changed between clients. Ink should be poured into individual cups for your session — never dipped from a shared bottle that has touched multiple clients. The shop as a whole should be visibly clean and organized.

Tennessee Requirements

WHAT NASHVILLE LAW REQUIRES the baseline

Artist Requirements

STATE LICENSING

Tennessee requires tattoo artists to hold a valid state license. You can verify an artist's license through the Tennessee Department of Health. A licensed artist has completed bloodborne pathogen training and meets minimum health and safety standards. Ask to see an artist's license if it is not visible in the studio.

Shop Requirements

HEALTH DEPARTMENT PERMITS

Nashville tattoo shops are inspected by the Metro Public Health Department and must display a current permit. Look for the permit when you enter the shop. Shops without visible current permits are operating outside regulatory compliance.

Age Requirements

18 AND OLDER

Tennessee requires all tattoo clients to be 18 or older. Any shop offering to tattoo minors — even with parental consent — is operating outside state law. Legitimate Nashville shops verify ID before every session.

Your Rights

YOU CAN STOP AT ANY TIME

You have the right to stop a session at any point if you observe safety violations or feel uncomfortable. You forfeit your deposit by stopping, but your health is worth more than a deposit. Any professional artist will understand and respect your decision to pause or stop a session.

FAQ

SAFETY QUESTIONS answered directly

How do I verify a Nashville tattoo artist's license?

You can verify tattoo artist licenses through the Tennessee Department of Health's online license verification system. Search by artist name to confirm current, valid licensing. Most professional artists will also show their license on request.

What should I do if I notice a safety violation during my session?

Tell the artist immediately and stop the session if necessary. You can also report safety violations to the Metro Nashville Public Health Department. Document what you observed with photos if possible.

Is it safe to get tattooed while pregnant or breastfeeding?

Most medical professionals recommend avoiding tattoos during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to infection risk and the unknown effects of tattoo pigments. This is a decision to make with your healthcare provider, not your tattoo artist. Most reputable Nashville artists will decline to tattoo pregnant clients.

What are the signs that a shop is not safe?

Visible dirt or clutter, artists not wearing gloves, inability to show you needles being opened from sealed packaging, no visible health department permit, and defensiveness when asked basic safety questions are all red flags. Trust your instincts — if something feels wrong, it probably is.
