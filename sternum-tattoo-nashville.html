The Placement

WHAT MAKES THE STERNUM UNIQUE

The sternum tattoo commonly called a chest centerpiece runs vertically along the breastbone and often extends across the chest or beneath the breasts in a symmetrical composition. It is among the most visually striking placements on the body precisely because of its central position and the way it frames the torso.

From a technical standpoint, the sternum requires specific design awareness. The skin directly over the bone is thin, making needle depth critical. The curved surfaces of the chest on either side of the centerline need symmetrical designs that account for the three-dimensional anatomy rather than treating the chest as a flat canvas. A design that looks perfectly symmetrical on paper can look uneven on a curved chest if the artist has not accounted for projection.

Sophie at Someone's Weird Sister has done significant sternum work, particularly botanical and ornamental centerpieces that complement the body's anatomy rather than imposing a flat design onto it. Natasha Rachel's fine line illustrative approach also works well for sternum pieces that need delicacy and flow.

Sternum tattoos are also among the more painful placements the bone proximity, skin thinness, and sensitivity of the area combine to produce an experience that is more intense than upper arm or thigh work. Clients who are pain-sensitive should account for this in their planning. See our pain guide for more placement-specific detail.

Design Considerations

WHAT WORKS ON THE STERNUM

01

SYMMETRY MUST ACCOUNT FOR ANATOMY

True anatomical symmetry on the chest looks different from mathematical symmetry on a flat page. Your artist needs to account for the projection of the ribcage and the natural asymmetries of individual bodies when designing a sternum piece. Stencil placement and mirror review are essential steps. Do not skip them.

02

VERTICAL FLOW WORKS BEST

Designs that follow the natural vertical line of the sternum and complement the chest's anatomy produce the most flattering results. Horizontal designs that fight the sternum's orientation often look awkward regardless of technical quality. Someone's Weird Sister's botanical and ornamental work demonstrates this flow principle at its best.

03

CONSIDER NECKLINE AND CLOTHING

A sternum tattoo is visible with V-necklines, swimwear, and open collars. It is hidden with crew necks and most professional attire. Think through your actual wardrobe in the context of where you want the tattoo to start and end on your chest. The design boundaries should be intentional relative to your clothing choices.

04

HEALING REQUIRES SPECIFIC AFTERCARE

The sternum area interacts with bra straps, seatbelts, and tight clothing during healing. Clients wearing bras need to plan the healing period to minimize friction on the work. Loose, soft fabric for the first two to three weeks of healing is the standard recommendation. See our full aftercare guide.

Pain and Session Length

WHAT TO PREPARE FOR

Sternum tattoos are painful by most clients' accounts, ranking among the more intense placements in the body. The bone proximity and skin thinness are the primary factors. Clients who are pain-tolerant typically describe the sternum as manageable with breaks. Clients who are pain-sensitive should plan for this placement requiring more session breaks than an upper arm piece of equivalent size.

Session length for a typical sternum centerpiece roughly eight to twelve inches from base to collarbone runs two to four hours depending on the design complexity and the client's need for breaks. Highly detailed designs will run longer. Budget the session time generously and let the artist pace accordingly.

Eat a full meal before the session. Stay hydrated. Let the artist know in advance if you are pain-sensitive or prone to faintness. The practical advice in our anxiety guide applies directly to sternum sessions. See Nashville pricing for what to budget for a piece of this scope.

FAQ

YOUR QUESTIONS ANSWERED

How painful is a sternum tattoo?

Among the more painful placements bone proximity and thin skin are the primary factors. Manageable with breaks for most clients, but significantly more intense than outer arm or thigh. See the pain guide for more specific context.

How long does a sternum tattoo take to heal?

Surface healing in two to three weeks. Deeper healing through week eight. The interaction with bra straps and tight clothing makes the first two weeks of aftercare more demanding than more isolated placements.

What designs work best on the sternum?

Vertical compositions that follow the sternum's natural line. Botanical, ornamental, and mandala-inspired designs are common. Fine line and illustrative work are popular. Bold traditional work can also be effective if sized appropriately for the placement.

Can sternum tattoos affect breastfeeding or chest surgery?

Tattoos do not affect breast tissue function. For clients who have had or plan breast surgery, discuss the timing and placement with your surgeon as well as your tattoo artist. Existing scarring from surgery affects how the area heals and needs to be disclosed at the consultation.

Does body shape affect how a sternum tattoo looks?

Yes. The chest's natural curvature and the projection of the ribcage affect how the design reads. A design needs to be calibrated to the specific anatomy of the individual client. A design that worked on one person's chest will not necessarily translate directly to another's without adjustment.

How much does a sternum tattoo cost in Nashville?

A sternum centerpiece of typical scope runs $400 to $800 or more depending on complexity and session length. See our full pricing guide.
