Nashville Through a Tattoo Lens

WHY NASHVILLE IS ONE OF THE BEST TATTOO CITIES in the American South

Nashville has become one of the most tattooed cities in the South over the past decade. The creative migration that brought musicians, artists, and entrepreneurs to Music City also brought serious tattoo talent. Today the city has artists working at a level that rivals shops in Los Angeles, New York, and Austin.

For visitors, this creates a genuine opportunity. Nashville is not just a place to get a cheap tourist tattoo on Broadway — it is a city where you can find exceptional work in almost any style from fine line specialists like Natasha Rachel to realism artists like Jake Ingersoll to bold graphic work from Sasha Vandal. The key is knowing where to look and booking before you arrive.

Before You Arrive

HOW TO PLAN A TATTOO INTO YOUR NASHVILLE TRIP without ruining your weekend

01

BOOK BEFORE YOU FLY

Nashville's best artists book 2 to 6 weeks out. If you are visiting for a weekend and want quality work, the booking conversation needs to happen before you buy your plane ticket. Same-week availability at quality shops is rare. The exception is walk-in flash at shops like Sunrise Tattoo — but even those fill up on weekends.

02

SCHEDULE IT EARLY IN YOUR TRIP

A tattoo done on Thursday heals better through a weekend in Nashville than one done Saturday night before a Sunday flight. The first 48 hours of healing matter most — you want to be somewhere you can follow aftercare instructions, not stuck on a plane or at a honky tonk. See our aftercare guide for what the first days require.

03

COME WITH A DIRECTION

Artists prefer clients who have thought about what they want even loosely. You do not need a final design — a style direction, a subject area, and a rough size is enough for a productive consultation. See our style guides: fine line, traditional, black and grey.

04

BUDGET CORRECTLY

Nashville tourist tattoo prices range from $80 minimum at walk-in shops to $300 plus for quality custom work. Plan for the full cost including tip — 20 percent is standard. See our Nashville cost guide for realistic numbers by style and size.

Where to Go

BROADWAY VS THE REST OF NASHVILLE an honest comparison

Convenient but Cautious

BROADWAY AND DOWNTOWN

High visibility, easy to find, open late. Also high volume, tourist-oriented, and variable quality. Walk-in Broadway shops are fine for simple flash at a reasonable price. Not the right choice for custom work, complex pieces, or first-timers who need extra care. If you go Broadway, stick to simple designs and know what you are paying for.

Where the Quality Lives

EAST NASHVILLE AND NEIGHBORHOODS

Nashville's best artists work in the city's residential neighborhoods — East Nashville, 12 South, Germantown, and Berry Hill. These shops are not tourist traps. They are working artists who serve locals and serious clients. Worth the five-minute Uber from downtown for significantly better work.

FAQ

TOURIST TATTOO QUESTIONS answered honestly

Is it safe to get a tattoo while visiting Nashville?

Yes, as long as you choose a reputable shop. Tennessee has licensing requirements for tattoo artists and health department oversight of shops. The artists on this site all operate in compliant, professional environments. The risks come from choosing unlicensed artists or shops with poor hygiene standards — which the Broadway tourist corridor occasionally has. Choose carefully.

Can I get tattooed the same day I arrive in Nashville?

Possibly for walk-in flash at Sunrise Tattoo or similar shops. For anything custom or with specific artist requests, same-day is not realistic. Quality artists do not have same-day availability. If same-day is important, your options are walk-in flash only.

What should I do with a healing tattoo in Nashville?

Keep it covered during outdoor activities, avoid pool and river swimming for 3 weeks, keep it out of direct sun, and follow your artist's specific instructions. Nashville summers mean sweat and sun are constant considerations. See our aftercare guide for Nashville-specific healing advice.

Is Broadway a good place to get tattooed?

For simple, small flash — potentially. For anything you care about long-term, no. Broadway shops are optimized for tourist volume, not for the kind of careful custom work that makes tattoos last. The quality range is enormous and difficult to evaluate on the spot. Our curated artists outside the tourist corridor are a safer investment.
