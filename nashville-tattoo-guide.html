FAQ

COMMON questions

How much does a tattoo cost in Nashville?+
Most Nashville artists charge between $150 and $250 per hour, with a shop minimum of around $100 to $150 for very small pieces. A palm-sized design typically runs $300 to $500. A half sleeve starts around $1,500 and goes up from there depending on the artist and complexity. Read the full Nashville tattoo cost guide for a detailed breakdown.
What is the best tattoo style for a first tattoo?+
American Traditional and bold blackwork are generally the best choices for first tattoos because the heavy lines and simple shapes age well and are forgiving of minor healing variations. Fine line and watercolor styles require more experience to care for properly. That said, the most important thing is finding an artist who specializes in the style you actually want. See our first tattoo guide for the full breakdown.
Do Nashville tattoo shops do walk-ins?+
Some do. Sunrise Tattoo welcomes walk-ins Wednesday through Saturday. Broadway shops near the honky-tonks also typically take walk-ins, especially for flash designs. Artists like Darlin' Cait work by appointment only. If you are visiting Nashville and want a same-day tattoo, check the best walk-in shops guide first.
How far in advance should I book a Nashville tattoo artist?+
For in-demand artists, four to eight weeks is a reasonable expectation. Some artists book out three months or more. If you are planning a bachelorette weekend or a specific trip to Nashville, book as early as possible. Walk-in slots are available same-day at some shops, but for custom work you will need to plan ahead. Read more in the booking process guide.
What should I know about tattoo deposits in Nashville?+
Most Nashville artists require a non-refundable deposit to hold your appointment slot. This is standard practice and goes toward your final price. Deposits typically range from $50 to $200 depending on the artist and the size of the piece. If you cancel with enough notice (usually 48 to 72 hours), many artists will allow you to reschedule and apply the deposit to your new date. The deposit guide explains everything.
Is it safe to get a tattoo while visiting Nashville as a tourist?+
Yes, as long as you choose a reputable shop. All licensed Nashville tattoo artists operate under Tennessee health department regulations. The risk comes from impulse decisions at low-quality walk-in shops near Broadway. Stick to vetted artists and shops, ask to see their portfolio, and make sure the space looks clean. Our tourist tattoo guide covers exactly what to look for.
