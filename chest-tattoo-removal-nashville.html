What Chest Removal Actually Costs

Size drives cost here more than almost any other single factor.

A small chest design, like a symbol over one side, falls into the small pricing tier most Nashville clinics use, generally $100 to $160 per session across 4 to 7 sessions. A medium piece covering more of the chest moves into the $150 to $220 range across 7 to 10 sessions.

A full chest piece or heavily shaded design moves into the large tier, often $220 to $300 or more per session across 11 to 15+ sessions, since both the treatment area and total ink volume are considerably higher than a smaller design.

How Much Does It Hurt on the Chest?

Most of the chest sits toward the more comfortable end of our pain by body area breakdown, thanks to muscle padding over the ribcage. The sternum, the bony strip running down the center of the chest, is the exception, since there is less padding directly over that bone.

Most clients find a standard numbing routine, topical cream applied 30 to 60 minutes before your session, more than enough for the fleshier parts of the chest, with extra attention if your piece crosses the sternum. See our numbing options guide for the full breakdown.

Full Removal vs Fading for a Cover-Up

Full clearance is realistic on the chest with a consistent session schedule.

The chest's wide canvas makes it a common spot people plan a cover-up for, since an artist has plenty of room to design around remaining ink. If your goal is new art rather than bare skin, our partial removal guide covers how targeted fading, usually 2 to 5 sessions, can prepare the area for a cover-up artist to work with.

If you are set on completely clear skin with no new tattoo planned, budget for the full session count above and see our consultation guide for what to expect at your first appointment.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to remove a chest tattoo in Nashville?
Chest tattoos range widely in size, from a small piece around $100 to $160 per session to a full chest piece at $220 to $300 or more per session. A medium chest piece typically runs $150 to $220 per session across 7 to 10 sessions, totaling $1,050 to $2,200.
Does chest tattoo removal hurt more than other spots?
Generally no, the chest is one of the more comfortable areas thanks to muscle padding, though the sternum itself, the bony strip in the center, is more sensitive than the surrounding chest muscle.
How many sessions does a chest tattoo typically need?
A small to medium chest piece typically needs 7 to 10 sessions. A full chest piece or heavily shaded design can take 11 to 15 or more sessions given the larger treatment area.
Can a chest tattoo be partially removed for a cover-up instead?
Yes. The chest is a common spot for partial fading ahead of a cover-up, since 2 to 5 sessions of targeted fading is often enough to prepare the area for new work.
Is the sternum area different from the rest of the chest for removal?
Yes. The sternum sits directly over bone with less padding than the surrounding chest muscle, so pieces that cross it may feel more noticeable during treatment than the rest of a chest design.
Is it easy to cover a chest tattoo during healing?
Yes, a shirt fully covers the treated area, making sun protection between sessions simple compared to more exposed spots like the hand or neck.
Do larger chest pieces cost significantly more than smaller ones?
Yes. A full or large chest piece covers considerably more treatment area with more total ink, moving it into the highest pricing tier, often $220 to $300 or more per session across 11 or more sessions.
