What Blowout Actually Means

Blowout means the treated area often extends beyond the visible tattoo edges.

Blowout happens when a needle deposits ink too deep, past the layer of skin where a tattoo is meant to sit, letting the ink spread out under the surface. The result is a blurry, less defined look around the original lines, and it's more common in amateur work where consistent depth control comes with training and experience that inexperienced artists haven't developed yet.

For removal, blowout matters because the actual ink coverage can extend beyond what's visible on the surface, which a technician needs to account for when planning treatment. This is one of the main reasons a close initial assessment is worth doing for amateur pieces specifically.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a tattoo blowout and does it affect removal?
A blowout happens when ink is deposited too deep and spreads under the skin, creating a blurry, spread-out appearance around the original lines. It can make removal less predictable, since the ink covers a wider, less defined area than the visible tattoo suggests.
How many sessions does amateur tattoo removal typically need?
It varies more than with professional work, generally 4 to 10 sessions, since amateur tattoos range widely in depth, consistency, and whether any blowout is present.
Why does amateur tattoo depth vary so much?
Inexperienced application, whether by machine or by hand, often lacks the consistent needle depth control that comes with training, which can result in some areas being too shallow and others too deep within the same tattoo.
How much does it cost to remove an amateur tattoo in Nashville?
Expect $100 to $170 per session across 4 to 10 sessions, totaling $400 to $1,700, with the range depending heavily on depth consistency and whether blowout is present.
Should I get an amateur tattoo assessed before starting removal?
Yes, this is worth doing more than with professional work. A technician can check for blowout, uneven depth, and any skin texture changes that affect how the laser should approach treatment.
Can scarring from an amateur tattoo complicate removal?
It can. Amateur application sometimes involves more trauma to the skin than professional work, and any resulting scar tissue is worth having a technician evaluate before starting treatment.
Is it harder to predict the timeline for an amateur tattoo?
Somewhat, yes. Because depth and technique vary more in amateur work, an initial assessment matters more here than with professionally applied tattoos, where technique is more consistent and predictable.
