Why Eyebrow Tattoo and Microblading Removal Takes Longer

Eyebrow tattoo removal and microblading removal commonly take 5 to 10 sessions, on the higher end of what you would expect for a small body tattoo of a similar visible size, covered in more general terms in how many sessions removal actually takes. The reason comes down to pigment composition. Many eyebrow and microblading pigments are formulated with iron oxide or titanium dioxide for long-lasting color, and these metal-based compounds do not always respond to a laser the same predictable way that standard black or dark tattoo ink does.

This is also where the same underlying factors that determine any removal price apply, just calibrated for a facial cosmetic tattoo rather than a body piece: pigment density, how deeply it was implanted, and your skin's own healing response all still matter here exactly the way they do for a shoulder or arm tattoo.

The Oxidation Reaction, Explained

One of the most surprising and alarming moments for clients removing eyebrow or lip tattoos is watching the pigment turn a strange color mid-treatment, rather than simply fading. This is called oxidation: certain metal-based pigments react with oxygen once the laser breaks them apart, which can shift a black or dark brown eyebrow tattoo toward orange, gray, or a noticeably different tone for a period during the removal process.

This is a known, generally temporary part of the process for pigments with this composition, not a sign that something has gone wrong. A technician experienced specifically in cosmetic tattoo removal will typically warn you about this possibility before your first session, and can often do a small spot test in an inconspicuous area first to see how your particular pigment responds before committing to full treatment.

Lip Liner and Lip Blush Removal

Lip tattoo removal, covering both older-style lip liner and newer lip blush techniques, generally requires fewer sessions than eyebrow work, commonly in the range of 3 to 6 treatments. The vermilion border, the delicate edge where lip skin meets facial skin, requires careful technique, and clinics experienced in this specific area typically spot-test before full treatment given the possibility of oxidation here as well.

As with eyebrow removal, the honest answer to "how many sessions will I need" depends on your specific pigment, how it was originally applied, and your skin's response, which is exactly why a real consultation matters more here than a generic online estimate, similar to the reasoning covered in what actually determines your removal price.

Aftercare Is Slightly Different Too

The core aftercare principles from the general removal aftercare guide still apply, gentle cleansing, avoiding picking at healing skin, and consistent sun protection, but facial skin requires a bit more care around makeup and skincare products. Most clinics recommend avoiding makeup, skincare actives, and unapproved lotions directly on the treated eyebrow or lip area until it has fully healed, and holding off on new permanent makeup application over the same area until healing is complete.

Some minor, generally temporary lightening or loss of eyebrow hair directly in the treatment path is also possible, since the laser can affect melanin in hair follicles as well as tattoo pigment; this typically resolves as the hair growth cycle continues.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many sessions does eyebrow tattoo or microblading removal take?
Eyebrow tattoos and microblading commonly need 5 to 10 sessions with an appropriate laser, though this depends on the pigment used, how deep it was placed, and your skin tone.
Why do eyebrow tattoos sometimes turn a strange color during removal?
Cosmetic pigments often contain metal-based compounds like titanium dioxide or iron oxide. When the laser breaks these apart, an oxidation reaction can occur, sometimes turning black or brown pigment orange, gray, or a darker shade temporarily during the removal process.
How many sessions does lip liner or lip blush removal usually take?
Most lip liner and lip blush tattoos fade safely over roughly 3 to 6 sessions, generally fewer than eyebrow removal, though pigment composition and density still affect the exact number.
Is cosmetic tattoo removal more dangerous than body tattoo removal?
It requires more precision because it is performed on delicate facial areas near the eyes and lips, but it is not inherently more dangerous when done by an experienced technician using appropriate laser settings and eye protection.
Should I shave or avoid tinting my eyebrows before a removal session?
Most clinics do not recommend shaving eyebrows before treatment, since the laser can treat the area effectively without it. Some minor, typically temporary hair loss from eyebrow hairs directly in the treatment path is possible.
Can I get new permanent makeup applied after removal, or do I have to wait?
Most technicians recommend waiting until the treated area is fully healed, generally similar timing to waiting after body tattoo removal, before applying new permanent makeup over the same area.
Does the age of an eyebrow or lip tattoo affect how easily it removes?
Yes. Older cosmetic tattoos have often already faded somewhat and had some pigment naturally broken down by the body, which can sometimes mean fewer sessions than a fresh cosmetic tattoo of the same density.
